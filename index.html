Amy Cheatle

Qualitative researcher at the intersection of HCI, craft studies, and sensory ethnography

My work explores how human sensing and computational systems mutually transform established ways of knowing and making, examining the situated practices through which sensory perception and action are accomplished. By investigating the social, distributed, and emergent nature of sensing at the intersection of HCI, craft studies, and sensory ethnography, my work offers valuable insights for understanding diverse sensory experiences and developing more responsive technological systems.
